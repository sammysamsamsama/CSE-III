About Us

We are a small team of friends who believe that the internet should be easy to access and available in as many places as possible.
So, we came up with an idea: google search through texting.

The Problem

The internet is an invaluable service to many people. Anyone with the access to Google has the ability to learn about practically anything from the current weather forecast, to how to fix a car, to how to save someone in critical condition. From menial to life changing information, access is important. While the Internet hasnt always been around and is only a fairly recent development, its become an essential part of our everyday lives. Weve become so self-reliant on the Internet to the point where half of our school assignments revolve around Internet usage. People of all ages have come to rely on the Internet: students in all grades, stay-at-home parents looking for projects to kill time while her children sleep, and even individuals who live alone and need instructions on how to build a cabinet.
Yet, as valuable as the information is, not everyone has access to it. This may be for many reasons, from being in the middle of a desert, to simply having no device to connect and interface with. They may even have the ability to interface with many different things but not have a direct connection to the internet. The problem we wish to focus on is how access to the internet might be too expensive for certain individuals, specifically access through phone services that are paid by the minute (minute phones). Accessing the internet on these phones can drain your savings and deter the user from using the internet.

Our Solution

What our team plans to do is to create a service which allows an individual to not have to use the internet in order to access it, more specifically, to be able to receive simple Google search results with little to no data. The service would let the individual send a term for the server to search on Google, and send the results of back to the user. While this by no means allows everyone to use google, it does allow many more individuals to access it.
Specifically, there is a phone number you text your results, and that SMS message is where the whole information exchange takes place. This is much more cost efficient as it is only a text message compared to full-fledged connection to the internet.

Educational and Social Value

The main part of this problem was meant to focus on pushing forth the social idea that we can all connect to - interconnectivity. As humans, we are naturally social beings. If we dont know how others are doing, that may damage us mentally and emotionally. The thought that one cannot connect simply because they cant communicate through todays medium of the internet is one that we fail to address with many of our innovations or inventions. However, this is something we cant do forever knowing how intertwined our daily lives are with the internet.
Many people who arent able to connect to the internet at certain times, or in general, due to monetary issues, regional issues, or a natural disaster/dangerous situation, will be able to use this service to access the internet for information which they would not be able to obtain otherwise. Instead of requiring direct access to the internet, which can be beyond a surprising amount of people, you are just required to send an SMS to our number, and you are given as much information as you need for a small monthly payment.
Having information available is helpful to any person, especially when it comes to education and knowledge. The internet is by far the biggest supplier of information worldwide, and having it at your fingertips is becoming more of a necessity rather than a privilege. This allows a huge group of people to have this asset available to them, and really does broaden peoples strengths and allows a more eclectic variety of information available to those affected, which is a huge game changer.
